Privacy policy

Last updated: June 2026

Who we are

Legit or Leg It is a trading name of Maltings Digital LTD, a company registered in England and Wales (company number 17253787). We operate the website legitorlegit.co.uk and provide used car due diligence reports to private buyers.

For the purposes of data protection law, Maltings Digital LTD is the data controller of the personal data described in this policy. ICO registration number: ZC167794.

Our infrastructure

We operate a serverless infrastructure using Cloudflare Workers. Data is processed in memory during your request and is not written to any persistent storage by us. When your request is complete, the data is gone.

We do not store reports. Reports are generated on demand, displayed in your browser, and not retained by us. It is your responsibility to save or print your report if you wish to keep a copy.

Our lawful bases for processing

We rely on the following lawful bases, documented in our Legitimate Interests Assessment, a copy of which is held in our compliance records:

  • Contract performance — you have asked us to run a check, and we process the minimum data necessary to do that. This includes steps taken at your request before you enter into a contract to purchase a vehicle.
  • Legitimate interests — where a seller is operating in a commercial capacity, we process publicly available information about that trading entity so that buyers can establish whether their statutory consumer rights would be enforceable against it. The data we process is already public, individual names do not appear in reports, and the processing is the minimum necessary for that purpose.
  • Crime prevention — used car fraud is a documented and escalating harm. Processing publicly available data about seller identity for the purpose of fraud prevention falls within the recognised purpose of preventing crime.
  • Legal obligation — transaction records are retained for 6 years as required by HMRC.

We do not use your data for marketing, profiling, or any purpose beyond delivering the service you paid for. We do not rely on consent for any processing described here.

What data we collect and why

When you use the free check:

  • Vehicle registration number — this is data about a vehicle, not a person. It is sent to the DVSA and TfL via our Cloudflare Worker, processed in memory, and discarded. Never stored.

When you purchase a report:

  • Vehicle registration number, listing URL, and asking price — processed in memory to generate your report, then discarded.
  • Payment information — handled entirely by Stripe. We never see, store, or handle your card details. Stripe collects your email address for their payment receipt. See stripe.com/gb/privacy.

When you contact us:

  • Your name and email address — used only to respond to your enquiry.

Information about sellers

Our reports include information about the businesses and trading entities selling vehicles. This information comes from public sources — primarily Companies House, the official UK register of companies, and publicly accessible review platforms.

We report this information at company level. Reports comment on trading entities, not on individuals, and individual names do not appear in report output.

If you are a seller and believe a report has presented public information about your business inaccurately, contact us at hello@legitorlegit.co.uk and we will investigate. Because reports are generated from live public data at the time of each check and are not stored by us, corrections to the underlying public record (for example at Companies House) will be reflected automatically in any future check.

How long we keep your data

  • Report inputs (reg number, listing URL, asking price) — never written to storage. Processed in memory and discarded.
  • Reports — not stored. Generated on demand and displayed in your browser only.
  • Email address — we do not collect your email address as part of the report purchase. Stripe holds it for their payment receipt.
  • Contact enquiries — retained until resolved, then deleted.
  • Transaction records — retained by Stripe and in our accounting records for 6 years, as required by HMRC.

Who we share data with

We do not sell data. We do not share it with third parties for marketing purposes. The following services receive data only to the extent necessary to run your check:

  • DVSA — receives the vehicle registration number to return MOT history data.
  • TfL — receives the vehicle registration number to return ULEZ compliance status.
  • One Auto API / Experian — receives the vehicle registration number to return vehicle history data including finance, write-off, theft and identity records.
  • SalvageGuide — receives the vehicle registration number to return insurance salvage records.
  • Marketcheck — receives the vehicle registration number to return historic listing data.
  • Brego — receives the vehicle registration number to return market valuation data.
  • Companies House — receives seller or dealer trading names to return company information from the public register.
  • Stripe — handles payment processing. See stripe.com/gb/privacy.
  • Cloudflare — our infrastructure provider. See cloudflare.com/privacypolicy.
  • Anthropic — generates your report from the data retrieved. Report content is not used to train AI models.

Your rights

You have the right to access, correct, or request deletion of any personal data we hold. Given that we collect minimal personal data and do not store reports, in most cases we will hold nothing beyond a transaction record in Stripe.

To exercise your rights, contact us at hello@legitorlegit.co.uk. You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk.

Cookies

We use only essential cookies required to operate the payment process. We do not use advertising or tracking cookies.

2. What our service is

We provide due diligence reports on used car listings and sellers. A paid report draws on the following sources:

  • DVSA MOT History database (official government data)
  • DVLA-derived vehicle identity data
  • Experian vehicle history data, supplied via One Auto API — including outstanding finance, insurance write-off category, theft markers, VIN confirmation, and plate change history
  • SalvageGuide insurance salvage records
  • Marketcheck historic listing data
  • Brego independent market valuation
  • Companies House, the official UK register of companies
  • TfL ULEZ compliance data
  • Publicly accessible review platforms

A report contains two kinds of content, and tells you which is which:

Findings are factual data points retrieved from the sources above, presented as received and attributed to their source.

Verdicts and assessments are our honest opinion, formed from the findings stated in the report and shown alongside them. They assess the transaction — whether your statutory rights as a buyer would be straightforward to exercise, whether the data holds together, whether the price is fair — and they exist to inform your decision, not to make it for you.

Reports comment on trading entities at company level. They do not make statements about individuals.

3. What our service is not

Our reports are not:

  • A statement about the physical condition, roadworthiness, or mechanical soundness of any vehicle — no data check can see the car in front of you
  • A substitute for viewing the vehicle, checking the VIN against the V5C, or a professional inspection
  • A guarantee that data held by third-party sources is complete or correct
  • Able to detect matters absent from the records we check — for example damage repaired privately without an insurance claim, finance with a lender that does not report to UK databases, or a cloned vehicle carrying false plates and VIN
  • Legal advice, financial advice, or a recommendation to buy or not to buy
  • A statement about any individual person's honesty or character
