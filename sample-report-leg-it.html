On the numbers alone this is a fair deal — £6,495 against an independent valuation of £6,600, on a clean car with believable mileage. But value includes what happens after you pay. The Consumer Rights Act gives you strong protection when buying from a dealer — satisfactory quality, a 30-day right to reject — and those rights are claims against a company. A company incorporated four months ago, where previous registrations at the same address have been dissolved, is a company whose ability to stand behind this car in a year's time is unproven. The car is worth the money. Whether the transaction is worth the risk is the real question — and the seller findings above are why this report says walk away until they are resolved.
Your protection
Section 75 — use it
Pay at least £1 of the purchase price on a credit card. Section 75 of the Consumer Credit Act makes your card provider jointly liable for the full purchase price if the car is misrepresented or not delivered. This is not optional advice. It is the single most powerful protection available to a used car buyer and most people do not use it.
If you proceed despite this report
A legitimate dealer takes cards. Pay at least £1 by credit card — and treat a card-refusing dealer as a flag.
Before you set out
Verify the dealer's Companies House registration yourself at find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk — and get written answers to the seller questions in this report first.
Ask specifically about any faults, advisories, or work needed. Get the answer in writing.
At the viewing
Check the VIN plate — driver's side dashboard, visible through the windscreen — against the V5C. Any mismatch, walk away.
Confirm the dealer holds the V5C, and do a proper test drive — streets, a dual carriageway, not just around the block.
Take someone with you.
Before you pay
Check the invoice is printed and shows the registered company name and number, matching the active Companies House registration. Handwritten, or stamp-only with a trading name — stop.
Check which company name appears on any warranty — it must match the invoice and the register.
Pay at least £1 on a credit card. No deposit until the seller questions are resolved in writing.
